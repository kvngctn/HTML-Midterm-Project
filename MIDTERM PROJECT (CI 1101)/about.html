About League of Information Technology Students

Our Story, Growth, and Commitment to Excellence


What is League of Information Technology Students (LITS)?

The League of Information Technology Students (LITS) was originally established under the name INTEL, which stood for Information Technology League. The name was later changed after the Student Affairs Office (SAO) rejected it due to its similarity to a well-known microprocessor brand. The organization was subsequently renamed LITS, reflecting its identity as the official student organization for Information Technology students.

LITS was founded in 2004 under the advisorship of Mr. De Guzman. Its first set of officers was led by Mr. Jherold Valdoz as President, with Mr. John Ryan Celis serving as Vice President. Together, they formed the pioneering leadership that established the organization's foundation.

During its early years, the organization faced several challenges. The Information Technology program had a relatively small student population, with only one section per year level. This resulted in limited membership and a restricted organizational budget, making it difficult to conduct large-scale activities and events.

At one point, LITS became inactive due to these constraints. However, in its third year, a group of lower-year students expressed strong interest in reviving the organization. Although Mr. De Guzman initially hesitated to continue his role as adviser due to earlier challenges, the students' persistence led to the successful revival of LITS. Following this renewal, the organization significantly expanded its programs, initiatives, and student engagement activities.

After its revival, LITS achieved a major milestone by receiving its first “Most Outstanding Organization” award at UE Caloocan. Since then, the organization has consistently ranked among the Top 10 Most Outstanding Organizations on campus, demonstrating sustained excellence in leadership, service, and student involvement.

Foundation, Challenges, Revival, and Excellence

Founded in 2004 under the advisorship of Mr. De Guzman, LITS began as INTEL (Information Technology League) before being renamed due to naming concerns. Despite early struggles caused by limited membership and funding, the organization was revived through the dedication of lower-year students. Since its revival, LITS has grown significantly and earned recognition as one of the Top 10 Most Outstanding Organizations at UE Caloocan, including receiving its first Most Outstanding Organization award.


Our Mission & Vision

🌟 Our Vision

To be a leading and exemplary student organization recognized for excellence in leadership, innovation, and service, shaping Information Technology students into competent and socially responsible professionals.

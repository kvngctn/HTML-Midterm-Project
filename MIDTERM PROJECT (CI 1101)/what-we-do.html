What We Do

Supporting IT students through peer collaboration, academic resources, and student-led initiatives that strengthen learning and community within the campus.


Our Mission

The League of Information Technology Students (LISO) is a recognized student organization committed to helping IT students succeed academically and grow as part of the university community. Our mission is to provide a platform for collaboration, learning, and peer support, helping students navigate their coursework and develop foundational skills in information technology.

We aim to create a supportive environment where members can share knowledge, exchange study materials, and work together on projects or activities relevant to their academic development. By focusing on collaboration and student-led initiatives, LISO encourages active participation and engagement while fostering a sense of belonging among IT students.

What We Do

  • 📘 Provide academic support through reviewers, study guides, and collaborative review sessions for IT courses.
  • 🤝 Organize peer-to-peer mentoring within the organization to help new members adjust and succeed academically.
  • 💡 Conduct small workshops or learning sessions on relevant IT topics, tools, and software skills.
  • 🎓 Collaborate with the College of Engineering Student Council, where our officers help prepare reviewers and learning materials for IT students.
  • 🌐 Promote a sense of community among IT students through group activities, informal meetups, and campus events.

Advocacy & Collaborative Efforts

LITS is committed to promoting initiatives that go beyond technical training. We focus on creating inclusive spaces for all IT students, encouraging women in tech, promoting cybersecurity awareness, and implementing sustainability-driven technology projects. These efforts not only enrich the student experience but also cultivate socially responsible future IT professionals.

Collaboration is central to our work. We partner with the College of Engineering Student Council and other campus organizations to create reviewer materials, facilitate peer tutoring sessions, and develop study programs that support academic excellence. Members also participate in inter-departmental projects, contributing technology solutions to campus events and community service programs. Through these collaborations, we strengthen networks, share knowledge, and leave a meaningful impact on both the university and the wider community.
